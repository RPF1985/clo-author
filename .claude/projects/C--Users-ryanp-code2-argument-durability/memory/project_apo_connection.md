The APO project (C:/Users/ryanp/code2/APO/) studies whether aspirational policy objectives expand war support while limiting accountability when unmet. The belief perseverance literature discovered during the argument-durability lit review is directly relevant to APO's theoretical framework — specifically to H2 (aspirational framing reduces incorporation of unmet objectives into success judgments).

**Why:** Belief perseverance (Ross, Lepper & Hubbard 1975; Anderson, Lepper & Ross 1980) predicts beliefs persist even after justificatory evidence is discredited. This could either support or challenge APO's accountability asymmetry claim depending on how reference-dependent preferences interact with perseverance.

**How to apply:** Need a plan to incorporate belief perseverance literature into APO's lit review, theoretical framework, and potentially its experimental design. Key papers: Ross et al. 1975, Anderson et al. 1980, Johnson & Seifert 1994, Lewandowsky et al. 2012, Wood & Porter 2019.
